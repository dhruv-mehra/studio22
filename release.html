Release

a NUWFA film

Written and Directed by Meezahn Senbetta

Produced by Patryk Kot and Mega Dafiaghor

Staring: Joette Waters, Curtia Torbert, and Daryl Anisfeld

What does it mean to emote in the “right” way? An eldy woman goes on a journey to release decades of bottled emotions.

Meet the Filmmakers

Patryk Kot

Co-Producer

Meezahn Senbetta

Writer/Director

I wrote and directed Release in response to a statement that was made by my grandmother a few years ago. One day she told me to “never, never, never say you’ll never.” She proceeded to tell me that four decades before, her life was hard, so hard that she couldn’t stop crying, so one day she prayed to stop and hadn’t since. Since that time, she has been feeling a tightness inside of her and no matter how hard she tried, she couldn’t cry. She told me all of this and then said to be careful what I pray for. I’m not a prayer, but rather a thinker and I couldn’t stop thinking about my grandmother’s words. I was curious if there was a way for her to feel relief without doing the thing she physically was unable to do. And from that thought came Release. This film provides an opportunity for people to ask questions and challenge their conceptions of what “good grief” and emotions look like. In addition to this pursuit, I wanted to pay tribute to the strongest woman of my life while at the same time provide a platform and voice for elderly female bodies which are so often characterized or minimized in the media. Release has been a continual learning process from an emotional and technical sense that I am very proud and thankful to have been able to create and grow with a cast and crew of incredibly talented and dedicated women.

Mega Dafiaghor

Co-Producer

Mega Dafiaghor is a Chicago based director focused on narratives that reveal the inner life of women in their varying contexts of life. Graduating from Northwestern University with majors in RTVF and Creative Non-Fiction, Mega crafted many stories that would go on to win accolades such as the Bindley Grant, Advanced Directing Grant, and numerous English and School of Communication Departmental awards.
