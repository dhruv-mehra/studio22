Boba

a Multi Culti film

Written and Directed by Hannah So

Produced by Scarlet Li and Charmaine Tan

Staring: Claire Kwon, Eliza Kang, and Al Herrmann

High school senior Margot Park is uncertain about her future, a sentiment illustrated through her changing relationship with best friend Piper Lee and all of their shenanigans within a bubble tea shop.

Meet the Filmmakers

Scarlet Li

Co-Producer

The three of us were in the same screenwriting class when Hannah wrote Boba. When we workshopped the piece, Hannah casted Charmaine to read as the Boba shop owner and me as Piper. It was so crass and funny, and even in its first draft the script perfectly captured the rawness and fickleness in a teenage friendship between two girls. And Hannah had done it so cleverly, centering the narrative around the Boba shop. I didn’t grow up in America; I grew up in Beijing. Before coming here for college I never knew the cultural importance of Boba and Boba shops to the Asian American community. And I’m so proud that Hannah created a such beautiful friendship, with the Boba shop bearing witness to its ebb and flow.

Hannah So

Writer/Director

"Boba" follows the coming of age of Margot Park and Piper Lee, two Korean-American high schoolers who have been inseparable for as long as they can remember.
I wrote this screenplay in my freshman year at college with the intention to try and portray the dynamic, witty, fierce, complex Asian females that I know in real life, characters that have been chronically underrepresented in the media. I never thought my little screenplay would see the light of day, but thanks to the help of many wonderful people, it became a reality!
I learned so much from this entire process and am so so so immensely grateful to everyone who helped me along the way - thank you to my wonderful boba babes (Scarlet, Charmaine, Claire, Eliza, Al, Shelby, Renee, Eddie, Coco, Emily, Rey, Hunter, Camille, Lawrence, MaryKate, Julianna, Tracy, Bruna, Peach, Evey, Sofia, Grace, Sylvia, Sascha, and Cameron) for being the best cast and crew a girl could ask for, thank you to our gofundme donors for all of your generosity, thank you Tealicious Bubble for letting us film in your store and for being so warmhearted, thank you MultiCulti for believing in me, thank you Studio 22 for screening my film alongside all of these amazing student works, and thank YOU for watching! I hope this film makes you smile. Here's to seeing more diverse narratives hit the big screens in years to come!

Charmaine Tan

Co-Producer

I remember being in screenwriting class when the first draft of Boba landed on my hands. It tells the tale of a great friendship both developed and broken in a boba shop. It’s my first time co-producing a student film and I decided to do this because I found the story very interesting and relatable, in the sense that I did frequent bubble tea shops with my friends and we did spend many late nights there catching up with one another. What made the experience even more fun was that I was working on this film with the director and co-producer, both of whom are my friends. We also had a really great cast and crew so it was overall, an amazing experience. Although we did run into some troubles throughout the whole process, I’m happy that the film is finally complete and I’m excited to see it.
