A Studio 22 Film

Written and Directed by Alex Tannebaum

Produced by Erika Rodriguez and Avery Powell

Three college-aged friends’ psychedelic search for a sense of self-worth.

I want to make (as we have yet to shoot this) a film that captures suburban kids in the odd post-teenager, pre-adult phase of life. As children, we’re impulsive, we do what makes us feel good. As we get older, we learn to think about our actions and avoid cognitive dissonance. On top of this, and specific to the suburbs, growing up in and around money, kids are constantly told: “You are special. You are lucky.” Of course, some kids do exceptional things, but those aren’t our characters.

So here is a slice-of-life story of a couple of rich kids with the impulses of children, but the need to rationalize of a couple of adults that think they’re inherently special. They need to feel special, even as they’re challenged by the world and each other. The style of the film was heavily inspired by Easy Rider, as it depicts a certain type of people in a specific time and a recognizable place—without taking a strong position on their actions. It revels in a sense of freedom, and specifically in Fly in the Ointment, the privilege of this freedom is examined through psychedelic fun and rock music over the course of a day in the life.

I wanted to write a short that would function well as a short, instead of a crunched up feature in 20 minutes. I wanted to root it in my identity, something I could say that no one else could or at least no one is. Stories like this, about the white and rich, should not necessarily be in the spotlight right now, but I think many people from a similar background will relate to the classic suburban day trip—going downtown to the same 2 square mile block with a few of your friends, just to feel a little something different.

–Alex Tannebaum
