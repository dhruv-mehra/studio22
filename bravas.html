Written and Directed by Erika Rodriguez

Produced by Jesse Cortez and Lydia Spettel

Starring: Maria F. Velazquez, Ainsley Soane, Mariela Stewart, Benjamin Rodriguez

Torn between her grieving mother and pleasing her upper-class peers, Estrella contemplates whether it’s time to start living a “normal” adolescent life or to keep being there for her mother.

Erika Rodriguez

Writer/Director

Bravas is a very selfish film. As a first generation Mexican American, my cultural identity was always something I was tested on by the people I grew up around. I was either “too American” to be Mexican, or “too Mexican” to be American - I was never just both. For a long time, I felt stuck. Who was I, if I could neither be from here nor there? It felt impossible to just be a singular entity. I wanted to highlight this feeling of being split in half, and conveying feelings between both sides of yourself. It’s very melodramatic and angsty, to be honest. However, there’s something about melodrama that is very endearing to me. It’s excessive, indulgent, and hard to tame at times. Being hard to tame, as the word “brava” implies, was my approach to shedding light on this experience. I hope you feel all the complexities of loving and caring for people who might speak different love languages than you - especially if the person in need of affection from you... is yourself.
Thank you to everyone who made Bravas possible. To my amazingly talented cast and crew, this wouldn't have gotten done without every single one of you. To Clay Mills, Thomas Molash, and Sarah Nogly for piecing my vision for this film together. To Jesse and Lydia, for being my incredibly hot producers, believing in me, and still encouraging me to do my best after watching me ugly cry about this project multiple times.
